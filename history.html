The Story of Talisay City

A Glimpse into the History of Our Heritage

The Beginning

A Symbol of Strength

Talisay City, located just south of Cebu City, is one of the province’s oldest and most historically rich areas. Its name comes from the “talisay” tree (Terminalia catappa), which once grew abundantly along its coastline — a symbol of strength and shelter for locals throughout generations.

1648

The Heart of the Town

Founded in 1648 by Augustinian friars, Talisay began as a small settlement. The Sta. Teresa de Avila Church became the heart of the town — both spiritually and socially. Around it grew a lively community known for faith, family, and hard work, prospering as a coastal hub for trade, fishing, and farming.

March 26, 1945

The Liberation of Cebu

Talisay holds a proud place in Philippine history. On this day, American and Filipino forces landed on its shores in a mission to liberate Cebu from Japanese occupation. This event, known as the “Landing at Talisay”, marked a turning point in World War II in the Visayas and is honored by the Talisay Landing Historical Marker.

January 12, 2001

Gateway to Southern Cebu

Over the years, Talisay has grown into a thriving urban area while retaining its small-town warmth. On this day, it was officially declared a component city of Cebu Province through Republic Act No. 8979. It is now known as the “Gateway to Southern Cebu”, balancing progress with cherished tradition.

Rooted in History, Growing with Heart.

Talisay City continues to be a place where historical landmarks meet modern parks, and where every lechon feast and seaside view tells a story of pride, resilience, and community.
